Silent Moon

Immersive meditation app with mindful design.
My Role
UI & UX Designer, Reseacher
Duration
2 Week
Tools
Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop
Overview
SilentMoon, a UI/UX Design Course Project, is a meditation app that encompasses guidance in mindfulness and self-care techniques while adhering to the style guidelines of Google and Apple. The app showcases a meticulous implementation of iconography, color schemes, and visual design, resulting in an immersive and visually captivating user experience.
Objective
The goal is to develop a user-friendly and visually appealing meditation app that provides a free library of audio clips for both Android and iPhone users. The app aims to cater to individuals seeking a cost-effective way to practice meditation and mindfulness, with a focus on improving mental and emotional well-being. By adhering to the distinct brand style guidelines, the app ensures a consistent user experience while delivering a valuable service to its users.
Design Process
Empathize
The project aimed to enhance UI/UX design skills by creating a meditation and self-care app for iOS and Android platforms. The app's primary focus was to provide a free library of meditation audio clips, supporting users in establishing a consistent meditation practice. The objective was to design a visually appealing and user-friendly app that offered a valuable service to individuals looking to enhance their mental and emotional well-being.
1.0 Research Mobile Platforms
To ensure a successful design for both iOS and Android platforms, thorough research was conducted on their respective design guidelines, including Android's Material Design Guidelines and Apple's Human Interface Guidelines. This research provided valuable insights into the specific typefaces, icons, color palettes, and UI elements utilized by each operating system, enabling a familiar and cohesive user experience. Armed with this knowledge, the design for the SilentMoon app was tailored to accommodate the necessary design distinctions for both versions, ensuring optimal user engagement and satisfaction.
1.1 Competitor Analysis
To guide the development of my meditation app, I performed a comprehensive competitive analysis of renowned meditation apps such as Headspace and Calm, examining their features, user interfaces, and content offerings. Through this analysis, I identified gaps in the market that my app could effectively address. While Headspace and Calm provided diverse meditation libraries and supplementary features like fitness and music, most of their content was limited to paid subscriptions. This highlighted the need for my app's core objective: offering a free library of meditations to fill the gap in accessible meditation content.
Define
2.0 User Flow Diagram
To plan out the app's design, a user flow diagram was crafted, outlining the essential screens and user navigation. This diagram served as a blueprint for determining the app's features and estimating the number of screens that would require design implementation. By visually mapping out the user flow, I gained clarity on the app's structure and ensured a comprehensive and intuitive user experience.
Ideate
3.0 Mid-Fidelity Wireframes
I utilised Figma to create mid-fidelity wireframes for my app, taking into consideration the specific requirements of both iOS and Android platforms. For iOS, I incorporated device-specific elements like keyboards, typefaces, and buttons using UI toolkits dedicated to iOS, Android platform design. The app's functionality was designed to remain consistent across both platforms, with the primary distinction being the adaptation of elements to fit different screen sizes. By ensuring a seamless user experience on both iOS and Android devices, the app caters to a wider audience while maintaining a cohesive design language.
3.1 High-Fidelity Wireframes
In order to develop a visually pleasing and user-friendly app, I used Figma to craft high-fidelity wireframes and an interactive prototype. Drawing inspiration from acclaimed meditation apps such as Headspace and Calm, I embraced a minimalist design approach adorned with serene graphics cartoon imagery to create a stress-free and intuitive interface. Through iterative exploration of diverse visual styles, I settled on a simple yet soothing color palette. To enhance the app's aesthetics, I utilized Adobe Illustrator and Adobe Photoshop to edit and refine vector graphics. Subsequently, I brought the designs to life by constructing functional prototypes for both iOS and Android versions using Figma.
Prototype
Testing
To evaluate the efficacy of my designs, I enlisted a select group of participants to offer feedback on the prototypes. SilentMoon was taken to include participants familiar with the respective operating systems being tested. They were encouraged to explore the app, focusing on aspects such as navigation, layout, and visual design. The feedback received was predominantly positive, with participants expressing admiration for the soothing imagery and color palette.
FUTURE CONSIDERATIONS
Since the beginning of the project, my main goal has been to create a user-friendly meditation app for both Android and iOS users. I'm excited to share that both versions of the app have successfully achieved this goal, offering a seamless experience, easy navigation, and visually appealing design. However, the biggest challenge I faced was choosing the right visual style for the SilentMoon app. After exploring different options, I decided to incorporate imagery of mountains, lakes, and night skies, as it felt most relevant to the app's purpose. This choice will be further developed in future iterations.
